## Charity Registration Process (UK)

### Option 1: Charitable Incorporated Organisation (CIO)

**Best for:** Small-medium charities, simple structure

**Advantages:**
- ‚úÖ Separate legal entity (limited liability)
- ‚úÖ Simpler than charitable company
- ‚úÖ No Companies House registration needed
- ‚úÖ Charity Commission only

**Requirements:**
- 3+ trustees (unpaid, volunteer)
- Constitution (aims, governance)
- Income >¬£5,000/year (to register)
- Annual accounts
- Charity Commission oversight

**Timeline:** 4-8 weeks from application

---

### Option 2: Charitable Company (Limited by Guarantee)

**Best for:** Larger charities, complex operations

**Advantages:**
- ‚úÖ Can employ staff
- ‚úÖ More formal structure
- ‚úÖ Better for growth and partnerships

**Requirements:**
- Same as CIO, plus:
- Companies House registration
- Directors (can be same as trustees)
- More complex reporting

**Timeline:** 8-12 weeks

---

### Recommended: Start as CIO

**Why:**
- Simpler initially
- Lower admin burden
- Easy to convert to charitable company later if needed
- Perfect for tech charity

## Risks and Mitigations

### Risk 1: Charity Rejection
**Probability:** Low (clear charitable aims)
**Mitigation:** Prepare strong application, seek advice from Charity Commission
**Fallback:** Continue as for-profit, reapply later

---

### Risk 2: Trustee Availability
**Probability:** Medium (finding committed trustees)
**Mitigation:** Start recruiting early, clearly define expectations
**Fallback:** Start with 3 minimum, add more later

---

### Risk 3: Regulatory Burden
**Probability:** Low (CIO is manageable)
**Mitigation:** Use charity accounting software, hire accountant for year-end
**Cost:** ¬£500-1,500/year (affordable at scale)

---

### Risk 4: Revenue Insufficient
**Probability:** Low (if user base grows)
**Mitigation:** Multiple revenue streams (subscriptions, donations, grants)
**Fallback:** Maintain low costs, seek grants, crowdfunding

---

### Risk 5: Mission Drift
**Probability:** Low (clear aims, trustee oversight)
**Mitigation:** Constitution locks in mission, annual reports show accountability
